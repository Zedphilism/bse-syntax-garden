### IDEEA

Analog and digital signals represent two fundamentally different ways of handling real-world information, where analog is continuous and digital is discrete.

### Details

- **Analog signals**
    - Continuous values over time
    - Infinite possible values within a range
    - Example: temperature, sound waves
- **Digital signals**
    - Discrete values (usually 0 and 1)
    - Limited set of values
    - Example: binary data in computers
- Key distinction:
    - Analog = smooth curve
    - Digital = step-like levels
- Digital systems are preferred because:
    - Easier to store, process, transmit
    - More resistant to noise
